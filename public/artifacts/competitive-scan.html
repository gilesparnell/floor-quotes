Competitive scan

The flooring visualizer market is crowded. The quoting workflow is the wedge.

Room visualisation, AR previews, iPhone floor-plan scanning, and product catalogue tooling already exist. Floor Quotes is stronger when positioned around quote-ready spatial commerce for local installers.

Recommendation

Build around lead qualification, measurement confidence, quote preparation, installer review, ordering, and job planning.

Market signal

Existing products already cover flooring visualisation from room photos, embedded retailer visualizers, manufacturer product catalogues, LiDAR room scanning, floor-plan generation, AR interior design, and ecommerce integrations.

That means a pure “upload a room photo and swap the floor” product is not differentiated enough.

Competitor landscape

Roomvo / Leap Tools

A major incumbent for embedded flooring and home-improvement visualisation across retailers, manufacturers, and distributors.

Floori

Covers product digitisation, visualizer, AR previews, kiosks, ecommerce integrations, analytics, dealer maps, and catalogue workflows.

Broadlume

A flooring retail selling system with in-store visualisation and broader retailer workflow tooling.

Mobile scanning apps

Magicplan, Matterport, Roomantic, SecondFloor, Draftwell, Alcovia, and similar tools already cover scanning and floor-plan outputs.

Where not to compete

  • Generic AI flooring visualizer.
  • Roomvo alternative.
  • Upload a room photo and swap floors.
  • iPhone floor-plan scanner.
  • AR interior design app.
  • Generic ecommerce flooring catalogue.

Potential wedge

The stronger product is a sales and quoting copilot for local flooring installers.

  • Customer uploads room photos or scans the space.
  • The system detects floor area, room shape, trims, obstacles, and complexity signals.
  • It estimates square meterage, waste, material quantity, underlay, trims, labour complexity, and quote confidence.
  • The installer receives a qualified lead with selected products, room evidence, budget signal, and constraints.

MVP direction

Web-first

Room photo upload, manual dimensions, small product catalogue, quote calculator, lead summary, and installer dashboard.

Later

RoomPlan import, USDZ viewer, AI-assisted measurement, floor-plan parsing, supplier feeds, multi-room projects, and quote confidence scoring.

The strongest framing is an AI spatial sales copilot for flooring installers: visually engaging, commercially tangible, and useful beyond the first preview.
