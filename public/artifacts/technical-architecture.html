Technical architecture

A shared project record connects intake, spatial evidence, quoting, ordering, and job planning.

The platform is not a simple left-to-right visualizer pipeline. It is a workspace where customer, installer, catalogue, spatial processing, quote engine, and commercial integrations all read from and write back to the same project record.

Architecture principle

Keep automation confidence-aware and installer-controlled. The system supports quoting; it does not pretend every measurement is final.

Supported inputs

iPhone RoomPlan

LiDAR-supported room scans can provide structured geometry, walls, openings, and model assets once the workflow is proven.

Room photos

Web-first uploads support the MVP, giving installers visual evidence and a path to AI-assisted floor-area detection.

2D plans

PDFs, sketches, and images can be parsed or reviewed manually for measurement extraction.

Manual dimensions

Customer-entered measurements keep the MVP practical before full spatial automation is ready.

System model

The project record is the source of truth for customer intent, room evidence, selected products, measurement confidence, quote assumptions, approval state, order pack, and job plan.

Each surface contributes to that record: customer intake adds evidence, spatial processing adds measurements and confidence, the catalogue adds product metadata, the quote engine adds assumptions and ranges, and the installer dashboard adds approval decisions and manual overrides.

End-to-end flow

  • Customer starts a project with contact details, address/suburb, room type, budget, preferred flooring, and room evidence.
  • Spatial capture or upload converts photos, scans, plans, and manual dimensions into a normalized room model.
  • Product selection pulls from installer or supplier catalogue data, including pack size, price, finish, install method, and accessories.
  • The quote engine combines area, waste, underlay, trims, adhesives, labour rate, preparation, complexity, and pricing rules.
  • The installer reviews assumptions, adjusts the estimate, and sends a polished customer quote.
  • The approved quote becomes an order pack and job plan.

AWS deployment

Web experience

CloudFront and S3 serve the customer and installer web app quickly.

Application services

ECS Fargate behind an application load balancer hosts project, catalogue, quote, user, and ordering APIs.

Async processing

SQS, EventBridge, and worker services handle scan parsing, image processing, quote jobs, PDFs, notifications, and supplier sync.

Data and files

RDS Postgres stores structured records; S3 stores photos, scans, plans, RoomPlan exports, and quote reports.

MVP scope

The first commercial MVP does not need full iPhone scanning or perfect AI measurement.

  • Web intake form, room photo upload, and manual dimension entry.
  • Product catalogue, quote calculator, and installer dashboard.
  • Approved quote to material order pack.
  • Rules-based job-duration estimate.
  • PDF or email quote output.

Risks

Measurement accuracy

Use confidence scores and installer review. Avoid making final fixed-price promises automatically.

Catalogue quality

Product data can be messy, so onboarding tools and supplier mapping matter.

Privacy

Photos and scans are sensitive customer data; retention, access control, and storage rules need care.

Supplier integration

Start with CSV imports where APIs are unavailable, then evolve into richer ordering integrations.

Customer input becomes structured project data, structured project data becomes a quote, and an approved quote becomes an order pack and job plan.
